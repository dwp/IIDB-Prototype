2. What you'll get

A ‘medical advisor’ will assess your disability on a scale of 1 to 100%. The level of your disability will affect the amount you're paid.

If you are an existing IIDB claimant, or have received IIDB in the past you will continue to be paid as you are or were, if you are a new claimant you will be paid 4 weekly.

You could get £33.60 to £168 a week depending on how severely your condition affects you.

You won't get your first payment for at least 91 days after an injury, but the amount you get will be backdated.

Normally you must be assessed as 14% disabled or more to get the benefit.
